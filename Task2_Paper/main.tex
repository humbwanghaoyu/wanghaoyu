\begin{abstract}
Predicting heart failure mortality is critical for clinical decision-making. This study compares a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) with traditional Logistic Regression. Using a clinical dataset (n=299), we found that Logistic Regression achieved a superior accuracy of 81.7\%, outperforming the MLP model (75.0\%).
\end{abstract}

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Data Preprocessing}
To ensure reproducibility, we implemented a median imputation for missing data and utilized \textbf{Stratified Sampling} (80/20 split). Features were standardized using Z-score scaling to facilitate the convergence of the MLP model.

\section{Discussion}
The superior performance of Logistic Regression suggests that simpler linear models are less prone to overfitting on limited clinical samples (N=299) compared to complex neural network architectures.
